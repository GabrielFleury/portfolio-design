Le Brutaliste

Le Brutaliste is a mobile game I worked on at NAD, made in Unreal 4 with a team of 21.
It is available on Google Play Store for free.

About:

In Le Brutaliste, you play as Howard, an angry architect in 1940s Montreal who seeks revenge against his rival, Peter. Tap and swipe as Howard runs forward to destroy as much of Peter's creations as you can!

My tasks:

Game design, level design on 3 stages, menu navigation, trailer

Video reflects the work of the entire team.

Plan of the level shown in the video above, annoted with explanations.

The basic rules of the game were already decided before I joined the project. My tasks were to "find the fun" and balance different aspects of the game to make it satisfying. I documented the heights and color codings of the obstacles and the rules of a level design for others to follow.

The tutorial

Both the programmers and artists were running out of time and we still didn't have a tutorial for the game. I took care of finding a simple way to explain the game without needing much extra resources. Instead of starting the game directly in Montreal, Howard begins in a 3D blueprint with tears open at the end of the tutorial to reveal a street with buildings.
The player gets introduced to mechanics with pop-up messages, then pictograms of the required touch motions are placed on the background, giving the players an early view of what to expect in a normal level in terms of pacing.
We had bigger plans for the tutorial, but this solution was better than no tutorial at all and was done by myself in a few days.
